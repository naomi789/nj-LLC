Before your wedding

I'm so excited to get started!

Create your wedding day timeline

You'll want to consider how big your wedding, family, and bridal parties are. The more people, the longer it will take to arrange groups of people for portraits, and the more portraits you'll want, so you can have different combinations of people. I talk through a few other things to keep in mind as you put together your schedule, which I recommend trying to finish one month in advance.

  1. Details

    2. I recommend creating a "bride bag" so all your details can be in one spot. This can be things like the wedding invitations, rings, perfume, shoes, dresses, scarves, veil, her hair clips, his watch, flowers, extra decorations, a gift from or to the bride/groom/parents... Something borrowed, something blue! Just let me know what is personally significant to you, and I'll do my best to get them in these detail shots.

  2. Getting ready

    3. By the time I've finished the detail shots, hopefully you'll have finished hair and makeup. I'll photograph the groomsmen putting on their boutonnieres and the bridesmaid helping the bride button up her dress or adjust her hair in the mirror. Some brides do a "first look" with their dad, mom, or bridesmaids.

  3. First look

    4. It's a relief to see each other when you have a long wedding day ahead of you! If you or your partner struggles with anxiety or gets nervous when they're the center of attention, this is especially helpful. If you have the budget for it, I recommend bringing in a second photographer at this point, so we can capture both reactions.

  4. Bride&Groom portraits

    5. After seeing each other for the first time, we can jump right into my favorite part of the day! Some people opt to do more photos during golden hour (the hour after sunrise or the hour before sunset).

  5. Wedding party portraits

    6. We can do all the permutations you'd like: bride and all the bridesmaids and groomsmen, bride and all the bridesmaids, bride with each bridesmaid individually, all of the above with the groom, and then combinations of groomsmen!

  6. Ceremony

    7. Before your guests come into the hall, I'll get a few shots of the details and the empty venue. Then, of course, photos of the wedding party walking in (tell the flower girl and ring boy to walk SLOWLY!), the bride and the groom, the officiant, and the big kiss! Most couples either do a quick peck, or hold the kiss until they get laughter, but you do you ;) I'm just hoping you'll kiss long enough that I can get more than one photo!!

  7. Family portraits

    8. Some weddings double as family reunions, in which case I'm happy to get a shot of all the aunts, uncles, cousins, grandparents, and siblings along with you, your spouse, and parents. But in the past decade, photos of you, your parents, spouse, and in-laws have become a much more popular request.

  8. Reception

    9. Like with the ceremony, I'll sneak in before the guests so I can photograph the empty venue and details like the refreshments. Let me know if there will be important toasts or other traditions, like cake cutting, daddy+daughter or mother+son dances, a first dance, or competitions between the brides family and grooms family! Some couples choose to have an evening send off with sparklers, or a day-time one with ribbons, bubbles, or rice.

  9. Any other shots

    10. If there's anything especially important for me to capture, please let me know! I won't judge you if you send me a screenshot from Instagram or Pintrest that you really want to re-create ;) If there are family traditions that you want to capture, or you're planning on surprising your new spouse with a gift, let me know so I can capture the moment. These will be photos you keep the rest of your life, so I want to make sure you get exactly what you want!
